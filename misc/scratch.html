Armed with a copy of GIMP and a "cutting-edge" desktop running Windows XP, my passion for design began at age 10, when I started making banners for users on gaming forums. Throughout my early life, I would constantly tinker with Photoshop as a hobby and would excitedly take up the opportunity to design visuals for school projects.

When I started college at UC Santa Barbara, my hobby turned into more of a passion. Upon joining my fraternity, I volunteered to be the Public Relations and Tech Chair. My responsibilities included creating graphics for events, managing the fraternity's social media presence, designing aparrel, and building the fraternity's website. Some of those designs can be found on the portfolio section of this website.

My work for my fraternity helped me land various jobs and internships, both in college and beyond. At their core, all of my roles had creation as their common denominator.
